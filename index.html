Introduction

Hello! I’m Gang Liu, a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate at the Kahlert School of Computing, University of Utah, conducting research under the supervision of Dr. Haitao Wang.

Educational Background

I began my Ph.D. program in the fall of 2021 at Utah State University. In the fall of 2022, I transferred to the University of Utah to continue my Ph.D. program, where I am now pursuing research in computational geometry and related fields.

Prior to starting my Ph.D., I received my M.S. in Computer Science from the University of Utah in 2020, and my B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Utah in 2017.

Research Interests

My academic research interest is in theoretical computer science (TCS), with the main focus on computational geometry, algorithms and data structures. More specifically, I am interested in coverage and hitting set problems. These topics of my publications can be found here. I am also passionate about solving problems in related areas and exploring their practical applications, such as sensor placement, geographic information systems, and resource management.

Work Experience

Before starting my Ph.D. program, I gained valuable industry experience working as a Software Developer at WaltUSA from July 2020 to July 2021. During this time, I contributed to the development of system solutions and worked on a range of projects that enhanced my skills in software design and implementation. Prior to that, I worked as a Strategy Data Visualization Intern at U Health in Salt Lake City, UT, from May 2019 to August 2019. Earlier, I interned as a Computer Vision Algorithm Intern at IFLYTEK in Hefei, Anhui, from March 2018 to July 2018, gaining hands-on experience in computer vision technologies.
